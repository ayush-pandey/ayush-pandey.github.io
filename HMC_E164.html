  • This is the course homepage for ENGR 164, listed as Introduction to Biomedical Engineering in the Engineering course catalog at Harvey Mudd College for Spring 2021-22 semester. The actual course title is Introduction to Biological System Design. This course is intended for juniors and seniors interested in the design of natural and engineered biological systems. After completion of the course, students will be able to:

    1. understand the basics of biological systems design
    2. develop mathematical models for biological circuits
    3. use numerical approaches to analyze biological data

    Course Description

    E164. Introduction to Biological System Design. Spring Term at HMC. 3 credits (2.5 hour lecture, 0 hour lab, 6 hour homework). Prerequisites: Basic familiarity with Python. Course Topics: Introduction to the broad fields of systems and synthetic biology by describing the basic biological processes, mechanisms, and components of biological designs. Particular topics include: gene regulatory networks, transcriptional and translational regulation, common motifs in biological circuit designs, chemical reaction network modeling for biological systems, mathematical analyses using differential equations, calculus, and algebra for biological models, and two case studies of biological systems. The course will be taught in Python language — so for everything that we will study we would like to use programming in Python to numerically or analytically understand the topics.

    Grading

    The final grade will be based on homework sets and a final project:

    • Homework (70%): The homework sets every week will comprise of one theory/short-essay/analysis problem and a few programming problems in Python. Each week the problem set will be out before the Tuesday lecture and will be due the next week on Tuesday before class. You can email your solutions in an ipython notebook format to me. Collaboration on homework problems is encouraged, please cite all sources in your submission (inlcuding any code snippets used from the Internet, research papers, or discussions with classmates). All homework submissions must be completed by each student individually that reflects your understanding of the material.

      The best 10 scores out of the 11 homework sets will be used to compute the final score for every student.
    • Project (30%): For the final evaluation, each student must work on a project of their choice for the second half of the course. The project will be evaluated on a final report for the project and a short 10 minute presentation during the last week of classes.
